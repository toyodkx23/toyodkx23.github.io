What is Judo (柔道)?

Judo is a Japanese martial art, combat sport, and Olympic sport that emphasizes throwing and grappling techniques to subdue an opponent. Founded by Jigoro Kano in 1882, its name translates to "gentle way," and it involves using an opponent's own force against them through skills like throws, pins, and submission holds like chokes and joint locks.

What is Digital Dojo?

Digital Dojo is an online platform where people who are interested in Judo—or who currently practice or have practiced Judo—can come together and train remotely. Through Digital Dojo, we aim to build a strong community and encourage participants to eventually meet and practice together in person.

Who is Hifumi Abe (阿部 一二三)?

Hifumi Abe is a Japanese Judo player who competes in the Men's half-lightweight (66 kg) division. He is a multiple-time world champion (2017, 2018, 2022, 2023).

Which national team won recent Olympic Judo mixed team gold?

Georgia won the mixed team gold, becoming the first nation other than Japan to achieve this feat since the event's introduction in 2017.
